What Did The Reference Sensitivity Analysis Do?

This page explains the last analysis step in plain language. It is not a new biological claim; it is a check that asks whether our frequency result survives reasonable preprocessing choices.

Main takeaway: after removing current candidate noisy channels and trying three reference choices, 180 amplitude / 5 Hz is still the strongest clean driven-frequency response. Among the 26 Hz conditions, 100 amplitude / 26 Hz has the largest positive 26 Hz-band power change under current preprocessing.

Two Different Questions

The confusing part is that `amp180_freq26` is strong in one kind of LFP measurement, but weak in another. Both can be true.

Broadband LFP response This asks whether the total LFP signal got bigger during stimulation, regardless of exact frequency. By this measure, `amp180_freq26` is strongest: sustained broadband change = 53.78.
26 Hz entrainment This asks whether power specifically increased at about 26 Hz during 26 Hz stimulation. By this measure, `amp180_freq26` is near zero/negative: raw = -0.062, shank median = -0.033, group median = -0.014.
Plain interpretation `amp180_freq26` makes the LFP bigger overall, but it does not look like a clean sustained 26 Hz-following oscillation in the current metrics.

The Analysis Flow

1. Start with LFP Use Dec 3 `amplifier.lfp`, downsampled to 1250 Hz.
2. Remove candidates Exclude channels 5, 6, 7, 32, 33, 34, 43, 66, 67 for this sensitivity pass.
3. Try references Compare raw, physical-shank median, and 32-channel group median.
4. Measure power For each condition, compare driven-frequency power during stimulation vs before stimulation.
5. Compare results Ask whether the winning condition changes.

What Is Referencing?

LFP channels share noise. Referencing means subtracting a shared signal so we can reduce common noise. If a result only appears under one reference, we should be cautious. If it appears under several, it is more robust.

Raw Use the LFP channels as recorded. This is simple, but keeps common noise.
Physical-shank median For channels 0-63 and 64-127 separately, subtract the median signal of that physical shank.
Analysis-group median For each 32-channel group, subtract that group's median signal.

What Was Measured?

Each trial has 3 seconds ON and 3 seconds OFF. For each condition we measured:

Driven-frequency power during stimulation divided by driven-frequency power before stimulation.

For 5 Hz conditions, the driven frequency is 5 Hz. For 26 Hz conditions, the driven frequency is 26 Hz. Positive numbers mean stimulation increased power at that frequency.

Why This Matters

Robust finding `amp180_freq5` is not just a raw-signal artifact; it remains strongest after candidate channel exclusion and median referencing.
26 Hz power candidate `amp100_freq26` becomes positive with median referencing. This means it is the largest positive 26 Hz-band power change among the 26 Hz conditions, not a final proof of entrainment.
Important caution `amp180_freq26` looked strong in broadband LFP, but this check says it is not a clean sustained 26 Hz power increase.

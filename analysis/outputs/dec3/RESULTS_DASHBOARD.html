Working Conclusion

Broadband LFP amplitude `amp180_freq26` is the strongest sustained broadband condition after excluding the first/last 100 ms of stimulation.
Frequency-specific power The cleanest full-window driven-frequency increase is `amp180_freq5`, not `amp180_freq26`.
26 Hz nuance Among 26 Hz conditions, `amp100_freq26` has the largest positive 26 Hz-band power change under current preprocessing; `amp180_freq26` is more offset-heavy.
Reference sensitivity Excluding candidate bad channels and median referencing does not flip the main driven-frequency result: `amp180_freq5` stays strongest.
Phase locking First PLV pass does not show strong trial-to-trial entrainment; sustained-minus-pre PLV changes are tiny.
Trial-level confidence Corrected bootstrap CIs support broadband effects for `amp180_freq26`; driven-frequency power CIs cross zero for all conditions.
Broadband transition story `amp180_freq26` has reliable sustained, offset, and recovery broadband effects; `amp250_freq26` is specifically offset-heavy.
OFF control The 3 s OFF period is also elevated for `amp180_freq26`, so the effect persists into recovery rather than being ON-only.
Adaptation `amp250_freq26` declines strongly over repeats; late `amp180_freq26` trials become OFF-dominant.
Spike sorting Prep metadata and SpikeInterface setup are ready: raw `.dat` opens lazily, 119 good channels, 30 s `tridesclous2` smoke test completed.
Cluster pre-curation Kilosort produced 194 clusters: 28 KS-good, 19 high-confidence by conservative automated rules, and 142 likely noise/multiunit candidates.
High-confidence spikes The 19 cleanest KS-good units still show no BH-corrected ON-vs-OFF unit/condition effects; mean deltas are near zero or negative.
Anatomy labels Use channels/groups for now. Do not assign medial/lateral or CA1/DG labels until probe orientation is confirmed.

Current result status: strong exploratory evidence, not final claims. The next analyses should rerun these plots after bad-channel exclusion and LFP referencing are finalized.

Important distinction: broadband LFP asks whether the overall signal got bigger; entrainment asks whether it got bigger specifically at the stimulus frequency. This is why `amp180_freq26` can be broadband-strong but not cleanly 26 Hz-entrained.
